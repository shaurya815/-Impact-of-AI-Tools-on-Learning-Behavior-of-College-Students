Abstract—Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools such as ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek and Copilot are increasingly being adopted by college students for academic purposes. These tools provide easy explanation in assignments, support coding tasks and research activities. With the rapid use of AI-driven platforms, traditional learning methods are undergoing some major changes, that is, students are no longer dependent on textbooks and classroom lectures but are actively integrating AI assistants into their daily study routines. Despite the huge productivity gains, critical concerns have been recognized regarding over-dependence and the use of independent problem-solving capabilities. In this study survey-based research on the impact of AI tools on the learning behavior, study habits and academic performance of undergraduate college students is done. A total of approx. ~a hundred students from different academic fields participated in the survey. The data was analyzed using statistics and percentage analysis. On finding that while AI tools measurably improve learning efficiency and time management, they simultaneously introduce risks of dependency and critical thinking. The study recommends a balanced framework for AI integration in higher education, one that preserves the foundational essential skills for long-term academic and professional success.

Keywords— Artificial Intelligence, AI Tools, Learning behavior, College Students, Educational Technology, Higher Education, Critical Thinking
